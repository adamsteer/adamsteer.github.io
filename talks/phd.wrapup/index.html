Why - part 1

The thickness distribution of Antarctic sea ice is very poorly described. Its variability at floe to sub-floe scales is unknown.

Why - part2

Sea ice is highly variable at sub-100m scales - which satellite altimeters cannot (yet) detect

Can we estimate sea ice thickness over areas big enough to compare with satellites, at a resolution comparable to field observations?
